Discover the Enigmatic Sri Lankan Leopards: Majesty in the Wild





The Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya) stands as a symbol of elegance and stealth amidst the diverse wildlife of this breathtaking island nation. Endemic to Sri Lanka, these magnificent big cats command attention with their distinctive beauty and elusive nature.





Habitat and Range

Found primarily in the island's dry zone forests, rainforests, and mountainous regions, the Sri Lankan leopard's habitat spans across national parks like Yala, Wilpattu, and Horton Plains National Park. Their adaptability to various landscapes, from coastal plains to mountainous terrains, showcases their remarkable resilience.





Physical Characteristics

Distinguished by their golden-yellow coats adorned with dark rosettes, Sri Lankan leopards are slightly smaller in size compared to their African counterparts. Their agile and muscular build, coupled with exceptional camouflage, allows them to navigate their habitats with grace and stealth.
