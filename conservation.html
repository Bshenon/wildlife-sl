Preserving Sri Lanka's Natural Heritage: Wildlife Conservation Efforts





Sri Lanka, with its stunning landscapes and diverse ecosystems, boasts an incredible array of wildlife. From majestic elephants to elusive leopards and vibrant birdlife, the island nation holds a treasure trove of biodiversity. However, preserving these natural wonders has become a top priority due to various environmental challenges.





Habitat Loss

One of the primary threats to wildlife in Sri Lanka is habitat destruction. Rapid urbanization, agricultural expansion, and deforestation have encroached upon the natural habitats of many species, leading to a decline in their numbers.





Human-Wildlife Conflict

As human populations expand into wildlife territories, conflicts arise. Elephants, for instance, often wander into farmlands, leading to confrontations that pose risks to both humans and animals.
